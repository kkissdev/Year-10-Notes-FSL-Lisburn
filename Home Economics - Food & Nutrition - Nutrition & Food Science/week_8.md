## Vegetarian and Vegan Diets

A vegetarian is a person who will not consume animal food for which has had to be slaughtered or has suffered in any way.

There are several factors which leads to an increase in vegetarianism:
- Disliking the taste of meat
- Being influenced by vegetarian family and friends
- Meat can be expensive
- Concerns for own health
- Religious beliefs
- Concerns for animal welfare
- Preferring the taste of vegetarian food

The three main types of vegetarians:
|Type|Description|
|-|-|
|Lacto-ovo vegetarian|- Will not eat meat, fish or poultry<br>- Will not eat products made from slaughtered animals e.g. lard (animal fat), gelatin (pig bone and skin) and suet (cow fat).<br>- Will eat eggs and dairy products e.g. milk, cheese, butter,cream and yoghurt|
|Lacto vegetarian|The same as lacto-ovo except the consumption of eggs. (Note the removal of 'ovo' in the name. Ovo = egg)|
|Vegan|- Very Strict<br>- Will not eat any product which comes from animals<br>- Meat, fish, poultry, milk, eggs, cheese and butter are excluded from this diet|

Vegetarians can eat the foods vegans eat but not vice versa.
