## Food safety

Food safety is about keeping food safe during preparation, cooking and storage, with the aim of preventing the contamination that leads to food poisoning.

### Food poisoning

What causes it?

- Cross contamination - bacteria from raw food being spread to cooked foood indirectly and directly
- Incorrect storage of food - temperature of fridge too high
- Eating food past its use by date
- Insufficient training of staff who handle food
- Not cooking food thoroughly
- Poor personal hygiene e.g. not washing hands before handling food

Foods that are more likely to cause food poisoning than others are called 'high risk':
- Meat
- Poultry
- Pate
- Seafood and shellfish
- cooked rice
- egg based products
- dairy, fresh cream or milk based products

#### E Coli

Symptoms:
- Nausea
- Vomiting
- Diarrhoea (may be bloody)
- Stomach cramps
- Severe anaemia
- Kidney failure

Sources: 
- raw meat
- undercooked mince meat
- gravy
- products made with unpasteurised milk (some cheeses)
- raw fish
- oysters

Method of control:
- prevent cross contamination
- cook meat and meat products thoroughly
- choose pasteurised dairy products


#### Camplyobacter

Symptoms:
- Diarrhoea (may be bloody)
- Stomach cramps
- Fever
- Vomiting

Sources:
- raw or uncooked meat
- poultry
- unpasteurised milk
- untreated water

Method of control:
- prevent cross contamination
- cook meat and poultry thoroughly
- wash prepacked salad leaves

#### Listeria

Symptoms:
- mild flu like symptoms
- nausea
- diarrhoea
- convulsions
- blood poisoning
- meningitis

Sources:
- unpasteurised cheeses
- soft mould ripened cheeses
- uncooked meats
- cold cuts of meat
- smoked salmon
- pates
- ready meals and ready to eat foods

Method of control:
- cook meat and poultry thoroughly
- wash raw veg before eating
- keep uncooked meat separate from all other foods
- choose pasteurised milk and cheese
- prevent cross contamination
- reheat ready made foods until piping hot

#### Staphylococcus aureus

Symptoms:
- severe vomiting
- diarrhoea
- abdominal pain

Sources:
- food made by hand that requires no cooking
- poultry
- cooked meats
- unpasteurised milk

Method of control:
- often lives on hands
- often lives in nose
- high standards of personal hygiene

#### Salmonella

Symptoms:
- Fever
- diarrhoea
- vomiting
- abdominal pain

Sources:
- poultry
- raw meat
- eggs
- unpasteurised milk
- raw unwashed veg

Method of control:
- separate raw food (especially poultry) away from cooked and ready to eat foods
- ensure chicken, eggs and other meats are thoroughly cooked until piping hot in the centre to above 63 degress centigrade
- always wash hands after handling raw chicken and other raw meats
